Why and When to Cite AI

Citing your sources is a core part of academic integrity. Just as you must cite books, articles, and websites, you must also cite Generative AI when you use it in your assessments.

Refer to the 6-Level AI Framework to know when you need to cite.

  • Level 1 (No AI): No citation needed because no AI is used.
  • Level 2 (Development): No citation needed, as AI is only used for general learning, not for the assessment itself.
  • Level 3 (Editing): No citation needed, as AI is only used for surface-level grammar/spelling checks (similar to using Word's spell-checker).
  • Levels 4, 5, and 6: Citation is mandatory. At these levels, you are using AI as an assistant or collaborator, and its contribution must be acknowledged.

Official University Guidance (Birmingham Newman University)

The university follows the Cite Them Right Harvard referencing style. As this style does not yet have formal guidance for Generative AI, the university has set the following specific policy.

1. In-Text Citation

When you quote directly, paraphrase, or use an idea generated by an AI tool, you must include an in-text citation with the tool's provider/name and the year the content was generated.

Example:

When prompted, the model explained that "generative AI works by learning patterns from vast datasets" (OpenAI ChatGPT, 2024).

Another approach to rehabilitation programming involves... (Google Gemini, 2024).

2. Reference List Entry

You must include a full entry in your reference list that details the provider, the date, the prompt you used, and the type of model.

Format:

Provider/Author (Year). Response to prompt '[Full text of the prompt you used]'. [Large language model]. Day Month.

Example:

OpenAI ChatGPT (2024). Response to prompt 'Explain the importance of the SARA model in sports coaching'. [Large language model]. 23 May.

Example:

Microsoft Copilot (2024). Response to prompt 'Summarise the key differences between biomechanical analysis of sprinting and long-distance running'. [Large language model]. 15 October.

Appendix and Documentation (Levels 5 & 6)

For assignments at Level 5 (Collaborator) and Level 6 (Expert), a simple citation is not enough. These levels require you to provide comprehensive documentation of your collaboration with the AI, which is usually placed in an appendix.

Check your module brief. Your lecturer will provide specific instructions, but this appendix will typically include:
  • The full transcript of your conversation (all your prompts and all the AI's answers).
  • A critical reflection on the process.
  • An explanation of how you edited, verified, and integrated the AI's contribution into your final work.
