SPU514: Investigating Sport, Exercise and Physical Activity

Level 6: Mandatory AI with Full Documentation

Assessment Overview:
  • Component: Individual Oral Presentation (60% of module)
  • Format: 8-minute presentation + 2-minute Q&A
  • Submission date: Monday, 23 March 2026
  • Presentation dates: 13th, 20th, 27th April and 4th May 2026
  • AI Level: 6 - Mandatory AI use with comprehensive documentation
  • Optional: Preregister on Open Science Framework (OSF) for extra marks

Understanding Level 6: Mandatory AI Use

At Level 6, you must use AI tools and you must demonstrate how you used and refined outputs throughout your work. This is not optional.

What Level 6 Requires

  • Mandatory use: You must use AI tools to support your research proposal development
  • Comprehensive documentation: Record every interaction with AI
  • Iterative refinement: Show how you improved prompts and outputs
  • Critical engagement: Demonstrate thoughtful use, not passive acceptance
  • Fact-checking: Verify all AI-generated information
⚠️ Critical: Failing to use AI or failing to document your use will result in not meeting the assessment criteria. Level 6 is about demonstrating sophisticated AI collaboration skills.

Assessment Requirements

Your Research Proposal Must Include

  • Study design clearly articulated and aligned with objectives
  • Theoretical framework integrated seamlessly
  • Sampling strategy appropriate and justified
  • Data collection methods clearly described
  • Data analysis plan thoroughly detailed
  • Credibility strategies (qualitative) or ethical considerations

AI Evidence Form Must Document

Component Weighting What to Include
Quality of Prompts 5% Clear, concise, well-constructed prompts tailored to research needs

Total AI documentation: 35% of your mark!

Step-by-Step Workflow

Phase 1: Topic Development with AI

Example: Developing a Research Topic

Initial Prompt (Good):

"I am interested in studying the effects of pre-competition routines on sports performance. Help me refine this into a specific research question for a sport psychology study."

Background Information Provided:

  • "I am a Level 5 sport science student"
  • "I have access to university athletes"
  • "I am interested in psychological factors"
  • "Study must be feasible within 3 months"

AI Response might include: Several refined research questions

Refined Prompt (Better):

"Based on those suggestions, help me develop a research question specifically about pre-performance routines in university-level team sports, focusing on anxiety reduction. What established psychological theories should I consider?"

What to Document:

  • Both prompts (show the refinement)
  • Background information you provided
  • How you evaluated the responses
  • Why you refined your prompt
  • Which suggestions you used and why

Phase 2: Literature Review Support

Using NotebookLM for Literature Synthesis

Process:

  1. Find 5-10 relevant articles from SPORTDiscus
  2. Upload PDFs to NotebookLM
  3. Use prompts like:
    • "Compare the methodologies used across these studies"
    • "What are the common findings regarding [your topic]?"
    • "What gaps exist in the research?"
    • "What theoretical frameworks are used?"
  4. Use Academic Rigour Assistant to verify key claims
  5. Cross-reference with original articles

What to Document:

  • Which articles you uploaded
  • Prompts used
  • How you refined prompts based on initial responses
  • How you fact-checked the summaries
  • Examples of where you found errors and corrected them

Phase 3: Methodology Development

Using AI to Develop Research Design

Initial Prompt:

"I want to investigate pre-performance routines and anxiety in university football players. What research design would be appropriate?"

Provide Background:

  • Target population details
  • Access to participants
  • Time constraints
  • Ethical considerations
  • Available equipment/measures

Refined Prompts might include:

  • "Considering I have access to 30 players over one season, would a within-subjects repeated measures design work?"
  • "What validated anxiety measures are appropriate for this population?"
  • "Help me develop a detailed data collection protocol"
  • "What are the ethical considerations I need to address?"

Critical Fact-Checking:

  • Verify suggested measures exist and are validated
  • Check sample size recommendations against published guidelines
  • Confirm ethical requirements with actual university policies
  • Validate statistical analysis suggestions with JASP capabilities

Phase 4: Statistical Analysis Planning

Using AI with JASP Knowledge

Good Prompts:

  • "I have repeated measures data from 30 participants across 3 time points measuring anxiety scores. What statistical test is appropriate and what assumptions must be checked?"
  • "Explain how to interpret Bayes Factors in the context of repeated measures ANOVA"
  • "What is an appropriate prior for a Bayesian repeated measures analysis in sport psychology?"

Fact-Checking Process:

  1. Verify statistical advice against JASP documentation
  2. Check assumptions against statistics textbooks
  3. Confirm interpretation guidelines with published examples
  4. Test suggested analyses in JASP with sample data

Document:

  • All statistical prompts
  • How you verified the advice
  • Any errors you found
  • How you integrated correct information

OSF Preregistration (Optional Extra Credit)

Preregistering your study on the Open Science Framework can gain you extra marks.

How AI Can Help with OSF

  • Understanding what information is needed
  • Drafting clear hypotheses
  • Specifying analysis plans precisely
  • Clarifying design elements

Using AI for OSF Preregistration

Prompt: "I need to preregister a study on OSF. Help me write a clear, specific hypothesis based on my research question: [your question]"

Prompt: "Convert my analysis plan into the specific format required by OSF preregistration"

Document: How AI helped clarify your preregistration, any revisions you made

Tip: Include your OSF preregistration link in your presentation to show advanced research planning skills.

What Excellent AI Documentation Looks Like

For 80-100% (Outstanding)

  • Prompts: Precise, well-tailored, show deep understanding of both AI capabilities and research needs
  • Iterations: Clear progression showing sophisticated refinement
  • Background: Rich, detailed context that significantly improved AI responses
  • Fact-checking: Exceptional validation with cross-referenced sources, errors corrected
  • Integration: Seamless incorporation showing critical assessment and enrichment

For 70-79% (Excellent)

  • Prompts: Well-constructed, effectively guide AI to useful responses
  • Iterations: Noticeable improvements through thoughtful refinement
  • Background: Relevant, sufficient context enhancing AI accuracy
  • Fact-checking: Careful validation with minor errors corrected
  • Integration: Effective incorporation with solid critical thinking

Common Mistakes Leading to Lower Marks

  • Vague, generic prompts
  • No refinement or iteration shown
  • Minimal background information provided to AI
  • No fact-checking performed
  • AI outputs used without critical assessment
  • Incomplete documentation
