General Questions

Q: Which pathway should I choose?

A: Choose based on your current level and assessment needs:

  • Foundation: If you are new to AI or need skills for Levels 1-3
  • Development: If you need Level 3-4 skills for essays and reports
  • Expert: If you need Level 5-6 skills for dissertation or major projects
Q: Can I use AI if my brief does not mention it?

A: NO. If your assignment brief does not explicitly state which AI level is permitted, assume Level 1 (no AI allowed). Always check with your lecturer if unsure.

Q: What happens if I use AI when I should not?

A: Using AI at a level not permitted by your assignment brief is academic misconduct and will be dealt with according to university regulations, which may result in a mark of zero or more serious penalties.

Tool Questions

Q: Why cannot I see the custom agents in Word or Outlook?

A: Custom agents only work in the main Copilot interface (copilot.microsoft.com or the Copilot app in Teams). They do not appear in the small Copilot pop-ups within other Microsoft applications.

Q: Do I need to pay for these AI tools?

A: No. All the AI tools mentioned in these pathways are free to use with your university login:

  • Microsoft Copilot (included with your Microsoft 365)
  • NotebookLM (free Google tool)
  • Custom agents (free to add to your Copilot)
Q: Can I use ChatGPT or Claude instead of Microsoft Copilot?

A: You can, but you must still document which tool you used in your AI Evidence Form. Be aware that different tools have different strengths and limitations. For academic work requiring citations, NotebookLM is usually safer than any general AI tool.

Level-Specific Questions

Q: For Level 3, what counts as the "original version" I need to submit?

A: The original version is the text YOU wrote BEFORE using any AI editing tools. Save this as a separate file before you start using AI to edit, and include it as an appendix to your submission.

Q: Can I use AI to write my first draft for Level 3?

A: NO. Level 3 is for editing only. You must write the first draft yourself. AI can only be used to improve clarity, structure, and grammar of text you have already written.

Q: For Level 4, can I just copy what the AI says?

A: NO. Level 4 requires you to critically evaluate AI output, verify it against academic sources, and substantially modify it. Simply copying AI output is academic misconduct.

Q: How much modification is "substantial" for Level 4?

A: You should be verifying every factual claim, rewriting in your own words, adding critical analysis, and citing actual sources. If your final text is very similar to the AI's original output, you have not modified it substantially enough.

Documentation Questions

Q: Do I need to submit the AI Evidence Form even if I only used AI a little bit?

A: YES. If you used AI at any level (2-6), you MUST complete and submit the AI Evidence Form. This is not optional.

Q: What if I forget which prompts I used?

A: Keep a log as you work! Write down your prompts as you use them. If you have already completed the work, try to recreate the prompts as accurately as possible based on your memory. For future work, maintain a document tracking all AI interactions.

Q: Where do I get the AI Evidence Form?

A: The AI Evidence Form should be available on your module's Canvas page. If you cannot find it, ask your module lecturer.

Verification & Fact-Checking

Q: How do I know if AI has "hallucinated" (made up information)?

A: Always verify AI claims by:

  1. Searching for the claim in academic databases (SPORTDiscus, Google Scholar)
  2. Reading actual research studies
  3. Checking if sources cited by AI actually exist
  4. Asking the Academic Rigour Assistant for verification
  5. Consulting your lecture notes and textbooks
Q: Can I trust statistics and percentages given by AI?

A: NO, not without verification. AI often generates plausible-sounding statistics that are inaccurate. Always find the original source for any statistic before using it in your work.

Academic Integrity

Q: If I document my AI use properly, can I use it for any assignment?

A: NO. Documentation does not override the level specified in your assignment brief. If your assignment permits Level 3 but you use AI at Level 4, that is still academic misconduct even if you document it.

Q: Is using AI cheating?

A: It depends on how you use it:

  • Not cheating: Using AI at the level permitted by your assignment brief, with proper documentation
  • Cheating: Using AI when not allowed, using it at a higher level than permitted, or failing to document your use
Q: What if other students are using AI more than allowed? Is that unfair?

A: If you suspect academic misconduct, you can report it confidentially through official channels. However, using AI inappropriately yourself because others are doing so is still academic misconduct and puts your degree at risk.

Technical Problems

Q: Copilot is not working. What should I do?

A: Check that:

  1. You are logged in with your university Microsoft 365 account
  2. You are using a supported browser (Chrome, Edge, or Safari)
  3. You have a stable internet connection

If problems persist, contact IT Services.

Q: NotebookLM says my document is too large. What should I do?

A: NotebookLM has limits on document size. Try:

  • Splitting long documents into sections
  • Uploading only the most relevant sections
  • Using PDFs rather than large Word documents
