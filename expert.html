Module 1: Understanding Levels 5-6

Core Knowledge

Levels 5 and 6 represent the most sophisticated use of AI in academic work. Let us understand what they require.

Level 5: AI Collaboration

What this means:

  • AI is used as a collaborative research partner throughout your project
  • You engage in iterative refinement with AI over multiple sessions
  • AI assists with planning, structuring, drafting, and refining
  • You maintain intellectual control and make all final decisions
  • The final product reflects substantial personal contribution

Documentation required:

  • Comprehensive AI Evidence Form documenting the entire collaboration
  • Evidence of iterative refinement across multiple sessions
  • Clear explanation of your role versus AI's role
  • Demonstration of critical evaluation throughout the process

Level 5 Example: Dissertation Literature Review

Collaboration process:

  1. Session 1: Use AI to help structure the review (themes, organisation)
  2. Your work: Conduct comprehensive literature search in databases
  3. Session 2: Upload sources to NotebookLM, use AI to identify patterns and gaps
  4. Your work: Read and critically analyse all sources
  5. Session 3: Draft sections with AI assistance for structure and flow
  6. Your work: Rewrite based on primary sources, add critical analysis
  7. Session 4: Use AI to refine arguments and improve coherence
  8. Your work: Final critical review and verification of all claims

Result: A literature review where AI supported the process but you controlled the content and analysis.

Level 6: Mandatory AI Use with Comprehensive Documentation

What this means:

  • The assignment requires you to use AI
  • The focus is on demonstrating sophisticated AI literacy
  • You must show expert-level documentation of your AI use
  • Often involves reflection on AI capabilities and limitations
  • May require you to evaluate the AI's performance

Documentation required:

  • Extremely detailed AI Evidence Form
  • Full transcript of all interactions (where feasible)
  • Critical reflection on AI's strengths and weaknesses
  • Analysis of where AI was helpful versus where it failed
  • Discussion of ethical implications of your AI use

Level 6 Example: Digital Training Resource Project

Assignment requirement: Create a digital coaching resource, using AI tools, and evaluate the AI's contribution.

Documentation includes:

  • Detailed log of all AI interactions
  • Analysis: "AI was excellent at suggesting exercise progressions but poor at understanding injury contraindications"
  • Reflection: "I needed to verify all safety information against professional guidelines"
  • Evidence: Screenshots of iterative refinements
  • Evaluation: "The AI saved approximately 15 hours but required 8 hours of verification"

Key Differences: Level 4 vs Level 5 vs Level 6

Aspect Level 4 Level 5 Level 6
AI Role Generate initial content Ongoing collaborator Mandated tool
Your Role Evaluate and rewrite Guide and refine throughout Use, evaluate, and reflect
Iterations Usually single session Multiple sessions over time Extensive, documented
Documentation AI Evidence Form Comprehensive Evidence Form Extensive documentation + critical reflection
Typical Use Short assignments Major projects, dissertation AI-specific assessments

When to Use Levels 5-6

Level 5 is appropriate when:

  • Working on extended projects (dissertation, major project)
  • The assignment explicitly allows Level 5 collaboration
  • You have time for multiple iterations and refinements
  • The work requires substantial research and analysis

Level 6 is required when:

  • The assignment brief specifies mandatory AI use
  • You are asked to evaluate AI tools
  • The focus is on demonstrating AI literacy

Module 2: AI as Research Collaborator

Advanced Practice

At Level 5, you work with AI as a research partner. Here is how to collaborate effectively.

The Collaboration Mindset

Think of AI as a:

  • Research assistant: Can help find patterns, organise information, suggest structures
  • Writing coach: Provides feedback on clarity, coherence, argument strength
  • Critical friend: Can challenge your thinking and suggest alternatives
  • Tool, not expert: Must be guided by your expertise and judgement

Effective Collaboration Strategies

1. Start with Planning

Use AI for:

  • Brainstorming potential research questions
  • Structuring your dissertation or project
  • Identifying key themes to explore
  • Creating work timelines

Example prompt: "I am writing a dissertation on mental skills training in elite sport. Help me structure a comprehensive literature review covering: theoretical frameworks, current evidence, methodological approaches, and practical applications."

2. Use AI for Research Management

NotebookLM for:

  • Organising and analysing multiple sources
  • Identifying themes across your literature
  • Comparing methodologies
  • Finding research gaps

Example workflow:

  1. Upload 20-30 key sources to NotebookLM
  2. Ask: "Identify the main theoretical frameworks used across these sources"
  3. Ask: "What are the common methodological limitations mentioned?"
  4. Use responses to guide your critical analysis

3. Iterative Drafting

Session 1: Ask AI to help structure a section outline

You do: Write first draft based on your research

Session 2: Ask AI for feedback on argument coherence

You do: Revise based on feedback and your judgement

Session 3: Use AI to improve academic style

You do: Final critical review and verification

4. Critical Dialogue

Use prompts like:

  • "What are potential weaknesses in this argument?"
  • "What alternative explanations exist for these findings?"
  • "How might critics respond to this position?"
  • "What evidence would strengthen this claim?"

Then: Research those points in academic literature

Sport Science Collaboration Examples

Example 1: Dissertation Methodology Section

Collaboration approach:

  1. You: Explain your research design to AI
  2. AI: Suggests potential limitations and validity threats
  3. You: Research those specific methodological issues
  4. AI: Helps structure your justification of design choices
  5. You: Write section citing actual methodology literature
  6. AI: Provides feedback on clarity and completeness
  7. You: Make final revisions and verify all claims

Example 2: Applied Project - Training Programme Design

Collaboration approach:

  1. AI: Suggests periodisation models for your athlete population
  2. You: Research evidence for different models
  3. AI: Helps organise training phases and progressions
  4. You: Adapt based on sport-specific requirements
  5. AI: Reviews for safety and progression logic
  6. You: Verify against professional guidelines and research
  7. AI: Assists with creating supporting documentation
  8. You: Final quality assurance and professional review

Maintaining Intellectual Control

Essential principles:

  • You decide the research questions and approach
  • You conduct the literature search and reading
  • You verify all facts and claims
  • You write the critical analysis and conclusions
  • You take responsibility for the final work

AI supports organisation, structure, clarity, and idea generation

AI does not replace your expertise, judgement, and critical thinking

Module 3: Iterative Refinement Process

Advanced Technique

Level 5 collaboration involves multiple cycles of refinement. Here is how to manage this process.

The Refinement Cycle

  1. Draft: Create initial content based on your research
  2. AI Feedback: Request specific feedback from AI
  3. Research: Investigate issues AI identified
  4. Revise: Make improvements based on feedback and research
  5. Review: Critically evaluate the revised version
  6. Repeat: Continue until you reach quality standards

Effective Feedback Prompts

For structure:

  • "Analyse the logical flow of this section. Where are there gaps or jumps in reasoning?"
  • "Does this section effectively support my thesis? What additional evidence would strengthen it?"

For argument:

  • "What are the weakest points in this argument?"
  • "What counterarguments should I address?"

For clarity:

  • "Which paragraphs are unclear or confusing? Why?"
  • "Where have I used jargon without sufficient explanation?"

For completeness:

  • "What important aspects of this topic have I not addressed?"
  • "What questions might a critical reader have that I have not answered?"

Documentation of Iterative Process

For Level 5, you must document your refinement cycles:

Example Documentation Template

Iteration 1:

  • Date: 15 January 2025
  • Task: Draft introduction section
  • AI prompt: "Review this introduction for clarity and logical flow"
  • AI feedback: Suggested the research gap was not clearly stated; recommended earlier introduction of key terms
  • Your action: Researched how other dissertations present research gaps; added definitions section; revised research gap paragraph
  • Outcome: Introduction now clearer but still needs work on transition to aims

Iteration 2:

  • Date: 18 January 2025
  • Task: Improve transition from research gap to aims
  • AI prompt: "How can I better connect the identified research gap to my specific research aims?"
  • AI feedback: Suggested using a bridging paragraph; provided structure example
  • Your action: Wrote bridging paragraph linking gap to aims; verified logic with supervisor
  • Outcome: Introduction now complete and coherent

Managing Long-Term Projects

Tips for dissertation-length collaborations:

  • Keep a log: Maintain a separate document tracking all AI interactions
  • Version control: Save versions after each major iteration
  • Regular checkpoints: Review progress with supervisor, not just AI
  • Balance AI use: Do not rely solely on AI; seek human feedback too
  • Ethical reflection: Regularly consider whether your use is appropriate

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls in Iterative Refinement

  • Over-refinement: Endless iterations without improvement
  • Loss of voice: Making so many AI-suggested changes that your writing no longer sounds like you
  • Uncritical acceptance: Implementing AI feedback without evaluation
  • Documentation lapses: Forgetting to record middle iterations
  • Verification failure: Not checking AI claims against sources

Module 4: Comprehensive Documentation for Levels 5-6

Essential Compliance

Levels 5 and 6 require extensive documentation. Here is what to include.

Level 5 Documentation Requirements

Your AI Evidence Form must include:

  1. Project overview:
    • Type of assignment (e.g., dissertation literature review)
    • Dates of AI use (start and end)
    • AI tools used (specify versions if known)
  2. Detailed process log:
    • All major prompts used
    • AI feedback received
    • Your responses and actions
    • Research conducted to verify AI claims
    • Changes made in each iteration
  3. Role clarification:
    • What AI did versus what you did
    • Which sections involved AI assistance
    • Where you departed from AI suggestions
  4. Critical reflection:
    • Where AI was most helpful
    • Where AI was least helpful or wrong
    • What you learned about AI limitations
    • How collaboration improved your work
  5. Verification evidence:
    • List of academic sources used to verify AI claims
    • Examples of where you corrected AI errors
    • Evidence of supervisor consultations

Level 6 Documentation Requirements

Everything from Level 5, plus:

  1. Complete interaction records:
    • Full transcripts or screenshots of AI conversations
    • Organised chronologically
    • Annotated to explain context
  2. Critical evaluation of AI:
    • Systematic analysis of AI's strengths and weaknesses for your task
    • Specific examples of successes and failures
    • Discussion of AI limitations in your discipline
  3. Ethical reflection:
    • Consideration of ethical implications of your AI use
    • Discussion of potential biases in AI outputs
    • Reflection on academic integrity issues
  4. Time analysis:
    • Estimated time saved by using AI
    • Time spent on verification and refinement
    • Overall efficiency assessment
  5. Recommendations:
    • How future students should use AI for similar tasks
    • What to avoid
    • Suggested improvements to AI tools or guidance

Example Reflection: Level 6

Extract from AI Evidence Form:

"Throughout this dissertation process, I used Microsoft Copilot (Academic Rigour Assistant) and NotebookLM as collaborative research tools over a 12-week period.

Where AI excelled: AI was particularly valuable for structuring my literature review and identifying thematic patterns across 45 sources. The NotebookLM citation feature meant I could trace every claim back to original sources, which was crucial for verification.

Where AI struggled: AI consistently oversimplified complex biomechanical concepts and occasionally conflated correlation with causation. I had to extensively rewrite any AI-generated discussion of research limitations, as AI tended to be overly optimistic about study quality.

Verification process: I verified every factual claim made by AI against primary sources. Approximately 30% of AI-generated claims required correction or nuance. I maintained a spreadsheet documenting this verification.

Time efficiency: AI saved approximately 20 hours in initial literature organisation and structure development. However, verification and refinement required an additional 15 hours. Net time saved: approximately 5 hours, but with substantially improved quality of critical analysis.

Ethical considerations: I was careful to ensure AI was enhancing rather than replacing my critical thinking. Regular supervisor consultations helped maintain perspective on appropriate use."

Module 5: Quality Assurance Protocols

Quality Control

When collaborating extensively with AI, rigorous quality assurance is essential.

The Quality Assurance Checklist

Before submitting Level 5-6 work, verify:

Content Accuracy:

  • ‚òê Every factual claim is verified against academic sources
  • ‚òê All statistics and percentages are correctly cited
  • ‚òê No AI "hallucinations" remain in the text
  • ‚òê All sources exist and are correctly referenced

Academic Integrity:

  • ‚òê The work reflects substantial personal intellectual contribution
  • ‚òê Critical analysis is original, not AI-generated
  • ‚òê All AI-assisted sections are clearly documented
  • ‚òê You can defend and explain all content

Writing Quality:

  • ‚òê The text maintains a consistent academic voice
  • ‚òê Arguments are coherent and well-structured
  • ‚òê No jarring transitions between AI-assisted and original sections
  • ‚òê Discipline-specific terminology is used correctly

Documentation:

  • ‚òê AI Evidence Form is complete and detailed
  • ‚òê All major AI interactions are recorded
  • ‚òê Reflection demonstrates critical engagement
  • ‚òê Verification process is clearly explained

The Verification Protocol

For any AI-generated or AI-assisted content:

  1. Identify claims: Highlight every factual statement
  2. Categorise: Which are general knowledge? Which need citation?
  3. Search: Find academic sources supporting each claim
  4. Compare: Does the source actually say what AI claimed?
  5. Document: Record source details in your reference list
  6. Cite: Add proper in-text citations

Red Flag Detection

Review your work for these warning signs:

  • Specific statistics without citations
  • Overly confident statements ("All research shows...", "It is proven that...")
  • Suspiciously perfect examples or case studies
  • Technical explanations that seem too simplistic
  • References that sound plausible but might not exist
  • Sections where you cannot explain the content in your own words

Action: Every red flag requires verification against academic sources.

Peer and Supervisor Review

Quality assurance should include human feedback:

  • Peer review: Ask a colleague to read sections and identify unclear areas
  • Supervisor consultations: Regular meetings to discuss progress and AI use
  • Writing centre: Professional feedback on structure and clarity
  • Discipline experts: Verify technical accuracy with lecturers

Activity 5.1: Quality Audit Practice

  1. Take a section of your work that was AI-assisted
  2. Apply the Quality Assurance Checklist systematically
  3. Identify any areas requiring additional verification
  4. Document your quality assurance process
  5. Make necessary corrections

Module 6: Dissertation Applications

Practical Application

Let us apply Level 5-6 skills to dissertation components.

Literature Review (Level 5)

Recommended workflow:

  1. Initial planning (with AI):
    • Brainstorm themes and organisation
    • Identify key theoretical frameworks
    • Create outline structure
  2. Literature search (you):
    • Systematic database searches
    • Find 30-50 relevant sources
    • Read and annotate all sources
  3. Synthesis (with AI support):
    • Upload sources to NotebookLM
    • Use AI to identify patterns and themes
    • Organise sources into structure
  4. Critical writing (you):
    • Draft sections based on your reading
    • Add critical analysis and evaluation
    • Synthesise across sources
  5. Refinement (with AI):
    • Request feedback on coherence and flow
    • Identify gaps in coverage
    • Improve transitions
  6. Quality assurance (you):
    • Verify all claims against sources
    • Check all citations
    • Ensure critical voice throughout

Documentation: Log every AI interaction; explain role of AI versus your contribution

Methodology Section (Level 5)

Recommended workflow:

  1. Design clarity (with AI):
    • Explain your research design to AI
    • Ask for potential limitations
    • Request structure suggestions
  2. Methodological research (you):
    • Research methodological literature
    • Find similar study designs
    • Read methods textbooks
  3. Writing (you with AI refinement):
    • Draft detailed methods description
    • Use AI to check clarity and completeness
    • Refine based on feedback
  4. Justification (you):
    • Cite methodology literature
    • Explain and defend choices
    • Address limitations proactively

Critical: Never use AI to invent or generate research methods you did not actually use

Applied Projects (Level 5 or 6)

For practical projects (e.g., training programmes, coaching resources):

Workflow example:

  1. AI brainstorming: Generate initial ideas and structures
  2. Evidence-based development (you): Research best practices and guidelines
  3. AI design support: Help organise components and progressions
  4. Professional verification (you): Check against professional standards
  5. AI refinement: Improve clarity and presentation
  6. Final quality check (you + experts): Safety and effectiveness review

For Level 6: Add comprehensive evaluation of AI's contribution

  • Where did AI excel?
  • Where did AI fail or need correction?
  • What verification was essential?
  • Would you recommend this approach to others?

Final Advice for Dissertation Success

  • Start early: Level 5 collaboration takes time across multiple sessions
  • Stay organised: Maintain detailed logs from the beginning
  • Consult regularly: Keep your supervisor informed of your AI use
  • Verify everything: Never submit AI-generated content without verification
  • Maintain your voice: The dissertation should still sound like you
  • Document thoroughly: Better too much documentation than too little
  • Be critical: Question AI outputs; do not accept them uncritically
  • Take responsibility: You are accountable for the final work, not the AI
