Module 1: Understanding Levels 3-4

Core Knowledge

Let us review what Levels 3 and 4 mean and how they differ from earlier levels.

Level 3: AI-Assisted Editing

What you can do:

  • Use AI to improve the clarity, structure, and grammar of text you have already written
  • The original ideas and content must be yours
  • AI is used only to refine your expression

Documentation required:

  • Submit your original unedited version in an appendix
  • Complete the AI Evidence Form showing what you asked the AI to do
  • Your submission should clearly show the improvements you made

Level 3 Example: Essay Editing

Original text (written by you):

"Plyometric training is when you do jumping exercises and it helps athletes get more powerful and jump higher and run faster because of the stretch-shortening cycle which makes muscles work better."

AI prompt: "Improve the clarity and academic tone of this paragraph while keeping my ideas intact."

AI-edited text:

"Plyometric training involves ballistic exercises that exploit the stretch-shortening cycle to enhance athletic power output. Research indicates that systematic plyometric programming can improve vertical jump performance and sprint velocity through neuromuscular adaptations."

What you submit: The edited version, plus the original in appendix, plus AI Evidence Form.

Level 4: AI-Generated Content with Critical Evaluation

What you can do:

  • Ask AI to generate initial content for your work
  • You must then critically evaluate, verify, and substantially modify this content
  • Your final submission should show significant intellectual engagement
  • You cannot simply copy-paste AI output

Documentation required:

  • Complete AI Evidence Form showing all prompts used
  • Explain how you evaluated the AI's suggestions
  • Show what changes you made and why
  • Include proper citations for any facts used

Level 4 Example: Literature Review Section

AI prompt: "Summarise the current research consensus on the effects of high-intensity interval training (HIIT) on VO2max improvements in untrained adults."

Your process:

  1. Receive AI's initial response
  2. Search SPORTDiscus for actual research studies on HIIT and VO2max
  3. Read at least 5-6 primary research articles
  4. Compare AI's claims against the actual research
  5. Identify where AI was accurate, oversimplified, or incorrect
  6. Rewrite the section in your own words using actual sources
  7. Cite all sources properly

What you submit: Your critically evaluated and rewritten section, plus AI Evidence Form documenting your process.

Key Differences Between Levels

Level What AI Does What You Do Documentation
Level 2 Generates ideas/topics You write everything yourself AI Evidence Form only
Level 3 Edits your existing text You wrote the original; AI refines it Original + AI Evidence Form
Level 4 Generates initial content You critically evaluate and rewrite Detailed AI Evidence Form

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Level 3: Using AI to write new content (not just edit)
  • Level 3: Forgetting to include your original unedited version
  • Level 4: Copying AI output without critical evaluation
  • Level 4: Not verifying AI claims against actual sources
  • Both levels: Incomplete documentation of AI use

Module 2: Advanced AI Editing Techniques (Level 3)

Practical Skills

Now let us develop your Level 3 editing skills with practical techniques.

Step-by-Step Process for AI-Assisted Editing

  1. Write your original draft yourself (no AI involvement)
  2. Save a copy as your "original version" for the appendix
  3. Identify specific editing needs:
    • Clarity and readability
    • Academic tone
    • Grammar and punctuation
    • Paragraph structure
    • Flow and transitions
  4. Create targeted prompts for each editing need
  5. Review AI suggestions carefully - do not accept everything
  6. Make final decisions about which changes to keep
  7. Document the process in your AI Evidence Form

Effective Editing Prompts for Sport Science Writing

For clarity:

"Improve the clarity of this paragraph about muscle fibre types while keeping my original meaning and ideas."

For academic tone:

"Revise this paragraph to use a more formal academic tone suitable for a Level 4 sport science essay, without changing the content."

For structure:

"Improve the logical flow and paragraph structure of this section on training adaptations, keeping all my original points."

For grammar:

"Check this paragraph for grammar, punctuation, and spelling errors. List the corrections needed."

Activity 2.1: Practice AI Editing

  1. Take a paragraph from one of your current assignments (that allows Level 3)
  2. Save the original version in a separate document
  3. Use Microsoft Copilot with one of the editing prompts above
  4. Compare the AI's suggestions to your original
  5. Decide which suggestions to accept and which to reject
  6. Document your process

Sport Science Editing Example

Original (your writing):

"Carbohydrate loading is important before endurance events because it increases glycogen stores in muscles which means athletes have more energy available during long races and do not hit the wall as quickly."

Prompt: "Improve the academic tone and clarity of this sentence about carbohydrate loading."

AI suggestion:

"Carbohydrate loading protocols are essential for optimising pre-competition preparation in endurance athletes, as this dietary strategy enhances intramuscular glycogen storage capacity, thereby improving substrate availability and delaying the onset of fatigue during prolonged aerobic exercise."

Your evaluation:

  • ‚úì Better academic tone
  • ‚úì More precise terminology
  • ? Perhaps too complex for the assignment level
  • Decision: Accept most changes but simplify "substrate availability" to "energy availability" for clarity

Tips for Effective AI Editing

  • Be specific: Tell AI exactly what aspect to improve
  • Edit in stages: Focus on one aspect at a time (clarity, then tone, then grammar)
  • Keep your voice: Make sure the final text still sounds like you
  • Do not over-rely: Your critical judgement is essential
  • Document everything: Keep records of your prompts and decisions

Module 3: Critical Evaluation of AI Outputs (Level 4)

Critical Thinking

Level 4 requires you to think critically about AI-generated content. Here is how to evaluate AI outputs effectively.

The Critical Evaluation Framework

When AI generates content, ask yourself these questions:

  1. Accuracy:
    • Are the facts correct?
    • Can I verify this information in academic sources?
    • Are any statistics or percentages cited with sources?
  2. Currency:
    • Is this information up to date?
    • Does recent research contradict these claims?
    • Are there more recent findings I should include?
  3. Completeness:
    • Has AI oversimplified complex topics?
    • What important nuances or limitations are missing?
    • What contradictory evidence exists?
  4. Bias:
    • Does this present a balanced view?
    • Are alternative perspectives acknowledged?
    • Does this align with the consensus in sport science literature?
  5. Relevance:
    • Does this directly answer my question?
    • Is this appropriate for my assignment level?
    • Does this fit with my argument?

Activity 3.1: Practice Critical Evaluation

  1. Ask Microsoft Copilot: "What are the benefits of beetroot juice supplementation for endurance performance?"
  2. Read the AI's response carefully
  3. Now search SPORTDiscus for research on beetroot juice and endurance
  4. Compare: What did the AI get right? What did it oversimplify? What did it miss?
  5. Identify any claims that need verification

Critical Evaluation Example

AI claim: "Beetroot juice supplementation improves endurance performance by increasing nitric oxide production, which enhances blood flow and reduces oxygen cost of exercise. Studies show improvements of 1-3% in time trial performance."

Your critical evaluation:

  • ‚úì Mechanism (nitric oxide) is correct
  • ? "1-3% improvement" - needs verification with specific studies
  • ‚úó Missing: Individual variation in response
  • ‚úó Missing: Dosage and timing protocols
  • ‚úó Missing: That not all studies show benefits

Your action:

  1. Search for recent systematic reviews on beetroot supplementation
  2. Find 3-4 primary studies with specific performance data
  3. Note the range of responses and protocols used
  4. Rewrite the section with proper citations and nuance

Red Flags: When AI Output Needs Verification

  • Specific percentages or statistics without source citations
  • Claims that seem too definitive (e.g., "always", "never", "all studies show")
  • Oversimplified explanations of complex physiological processes
  • Recommendations that contradict your lecture notes
  • Information about very recent research or events
  • Medical or supplementation advice without caveats

The Verification Process

For Level 4 work, you must verify AI claims:

  1. Search academic databases: SPORTDiscus, PubMed, Google Scholar
  2. Find primary sources: Look for original research studies, not just summaries
  3. Read critically: Check methods, sample sizes, limitations
  4. Compare multiple sources: Do several studies agree?
  5. Cite properly: Reference the actual studies, not the AI

Module 4: Advanced Research with NotebookLM

Research Skills

NotebookLM becomes more powerful when you use it for sophisticated research tasks.

Advanced NotebookLM Techniques

1. Multi-Source Synthesis

Upload multiple research articles to NotebookLM and ask it to compare and synthesise:

Upload: 3-4 research articles on the same topic (e.g., sleep and athletic performance)

Prompts:

  • "Compare the methodologies used across these studies"
  • "What are the main points of agreement between these sources?"
  • "Where do these studies disagree or show conflicting results?"
  • "What are the limitations mentioned in each study?"

Use for: Literature reviews, critically comparing research approaches

2. Identifying Research Gaps

Prompt: "Based on the limitations sections of these studies, what future research questions do the authors suggest?"

Use for: Identifying areas for further investigation in your discipline

3. Concept Mapping

Prompt: "Create a hierarchical outline of the main concepts discussed across all uploaded sources"

Use for: Organising complex information for essays or presentations

4. Critical Analysis Preparation

Prompts:

  • "What are the theoretical frameworks used in these studies?"
  • "How do the sample characteristics compare across studies?"
  • "What are the strengths and weaknesses of each study's methodology?"

Use for: Preparing for critical appraisal assignments

Activity 4.1: Advanced Literature Analysis

  1. Find 3 research articles on a topic relevant to your current studies
  2. Upload all 3 to a new NotebookLM notebook
  3. Use the prompts above to analyse and compare them
  4. Create a one-page synthesis showing:
    • Main findings from each study
    • Points of agreement and disagreement
    • Methodological differences
    • Gaps for future research

Why NotebookLM is Valuable for Level 4 Work

NotebookLM is particularly useful for Level 4 because:

  • It shows you exactly where information comes from (citations)
  • It only uses your uploaded sources (no hallucination)
  • It helps you identify patterns across multiple sources
  • It supports critical analysis rather than just summarisation

Remember: NotebookLM helps you analyse sources, but you still need to write your own synthesis with proper citations.

Module 5: Using the Academic Rigour Assistant

Quality Control

The Academic Rigour Assistant is a custom Copilot agent designed to provide evidence-based, carefully considered responses for academic work.

What Makes the Academic Rigour Assistant Different?

  • Evidence-first approach: Focuses on research evidence and theoretical frameworks
  • Cautious language: Uses appropriate academic hedging (e.g., "research suggests", "evidence indicates")
  • Acknowledges limitations: Explicitly discusses what is not known or uncertain
  • Discipline-appropriate: Understands sport science contexts and terminology

When to Use the Academic Rigour Assistant

  • Checking the accuracy of claims before including them in your work
  • Understanding the evidence base for a topic
  • Exploring the limitations of current research
  • Developing your critical thinking about sport science topics

Accessing the Academic Rigour Assistant

  1. Log in to Microsoft 365 with your university account
  2. Go to copilot.microsoft.com or open Copilot in Teams
  3. Click this link to add the agent: Add Academic Rigour Assistant
  4. Select it from your list of agents before asking questions

Example Prompts for the Academic Rigour Assistant

For understanding evidence:

  • "What is the research evidence for foam rolling improving recovery?"
  • "What are the main theories explaining the acute effects of static stretching on performance?"

For exploring limitations:

  • "What are the limitations of current research on mental imagery in sport?"
  • "What is still uncertain about caffeine supplementation and endurance performance?"

For fact-checking:

  • "Is it accurate to say that high-intensity training always improves VO2max?"
  • "How reliable is heart rate variability as a measure of recovery status?"

Activity 5.1: Compare Responses

  1. Ask standard Microsoft Copilot: "Does ice bathing improve recovery after exercise?"
  2. Note the response style and content
  3. Now ask the Academic Rigour Assistant the same question
  4. Compare the responses:
    • Which is more nuanced?
    • Which acknowledges limitations?
    • Which would be more useful for Level 4 academic work?

Using the Academic Rigour Assistant in Your Workflow

Step 1: Ask the Academic Rigour Assistant about your topic to understand the evidence base

Step 2: Use the response to guide your literature search (what terms to search, what studies to look for)

Step 3: Find and read the actual research studies

Step 4: Write your work based on the primary sources, not the AI

Remember: The Academic Rigour Assistant is a research guide, not a substitute for reading actual research.

Module 6: Documentation Requirements for Levels 3-4

Essential Compliance

Comprehensive documentation is crucial for Levels 3 and 4. Here is exactly what you need to provide.

Level 3 Documentation Checklist

You must submit:

  1. Your final edited work (the main submission)
  2. Your original unedited version in an appendix
  3. Completed AI Evidence Form including:
    • Exact prompts you used for editing
    • Which sections were edited with AI
    • What type of editing was requested (clarity, grammar, tone, etc.)
    • Which AI tool you used (e.g., Microsoft Copilot)

Level 4 Documentation Checklist

You must submit:

  1. Your final work with proper citations
  2. Completed AI Evidence Form including:
    • All prompts used to generate initial content
    • Description of how you evaluated the AI output
    • Explanation of what you changed and why
    • List of academic sources you used to verify AI claims
    • Reflection on the AI's strengths and limitations for this task

Example Documentation: Level 3

AI Evidence Form Extract:

Section edited: Literature Review, paragraphs 3-5

AI tool used: Microsoft Copilot

Prompts used:

  1. "Improve the academic tone of this paragraph about muscle adaptations"
  2. "Improve the logical flow between these three paragraphs"
  3. "Check this section for grammar and punctuation errors"

Changes accepted: Improved transition sentences between paragraphs, corrected three grammar errors, replaced informal language with academic equivalents

Changes rejected: AI suggestion to use overly complex terminology that would reduce clarity

Example Documentation: Level 4

AI Evidence Form Extract:

Task: Literature review section on protein supplementation timing

AI tool used: Microsoft Copilot (Academic Rigour Assistant)

Initial prompt: "What does research say about protein timing for muscle protein synthesis?"

Evaluation process:

  1. AI provided an overview suggesting timing is crucial
  2. Searched SPORTDiscus: "protein timing muscle protein synthesis"
  3. Found and read 6 recent studies and 2 systematic reviews
  4. Discovered AI oversimplified: recent research shows timing less important than total daily protein
  5. Rewrote section to reflect actual research consensus
  6. Included citations to: Schoenfeld et al. (2017), Morton et al. (2018), and others

Final outcome: Used AI as initial orientation but final content based entirely on primary sources

‚ö†Ô∏è Documentation Failures

Incomplete documentation may be treated as academic misconduct. Common failures include:

  • Not submitting the original unedited version (Level 3)
  • Vague descriptions of AI use (e.g., "used AI to help with writing")
  • Not listing all prompts used
  • Not explaining how you evaluated AI output (Level 4)
  • Submitting work that is too close to AI output without sufficient modification

Module 7: Applying to Your Assessments

Practical Application

Let us apply these Level 3-4 skills to your actual assessments.

Which Assessments Use Levels 3-4?

Check your assignment briefs. Common examples include:

  • Level 3: Essays, reports, reflective writing where editing is permitted
  • Level 4: Literature reviews, research proposals, case study analyses

Level 3 Workflow: Essay Editing

Step-by-step process:

  1. Write your full first draft (no AI - just you)
  2. Save this as "Original_Draft.docx"
  3. Identify sections needing editing (unclear paragraphs, awkward phrasing)
  4. Create specific editing prompts
  5. Use Copilot to get editing suggestions
  6. Review each suggestion - accept, reject, or modify
  7. Document your process in the AI Evidence Form
  8. Submit: Final essay + Original_Draft in appendix + AI Evidence Form

Level 4 Workflow: Literature Review

Step-by-step process:

  1. Use Academic Rigour Assistant to understand the topic
  2. Search academic databases for primary sources
  3. Upload sources to NotebookLM for analysis
  4. Read and annotate the actual research articles
  5. Create an outline of your review structure
  6. Write your review based on the sources (not AI)
  7. Use AI only for editing (Level 3) if needed
  8. Document comprehensively in AI Evidence Form
  9. Submit: Review with citations + detailed AI Evidence Form

Quick Decision Guide

Should I use Level 3 or Level 4 for this task?

  • Use Level 3 if: You have already written the content and just need editing help
  • Use Level 4 if: You need help generating initial content but will critically evaluate and rewrite it
  • Use neither if: The assignment brief specifies Level 1 (no AI) or Level 2 (ideas only)

When in doubt: Ask your lecturer before submission
