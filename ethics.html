Why Ethics Matter

Generative AI tools are not neutral. They are created by humans and trained on vast amounts of data from the internet. This means they inherit human biases, stereotypes, and potential inaccuracies. As a student and future professional, it is your responsibility to use these tools with a critical and ethical mindset.

1. Bias in AI

AI models learn by identifying patterns in data. If the data they are trained on reflects historical or societal biases, the AI will learn and often amplify those biases.

  • Stereotyping: An AI might associate certain jobs with specific genders (e.g., "all nurses are female, all engineers are male") or produce culturally insensitive content.
  • Lack of Representation: If an AI is trained primarily on Western data, it may fail to understand or accurately represent non-Western cultures, concepts, or contexts.
  • "Average" Answers: AI tends to provide the most common or "average" answer, which can stifle novel ideas and erase minority viewpoints.
Your Action: Always critically evaluate AI-generated content. Ask yourself: "Is this answer fair? Does it make assumptions? Is it missing an important perspective?" Challenge the AI by asking for alternative viewpoints.

2. Data Privacy and Security

Public AI tools (like the free versions of ChatGPT, Gemini, or Claude) often use your conversations to train their models. This means any information you paste into the chat could potentially be seen by researchers or used in future responses.

NEVER input personal or sensitive information into a public AI tool. This includes:
  • Your own (or anyone else's) personal data (name, address, student ID).
  • Confidential research data (e.g., interview transcripts, participant information).
  • Unpublished work or sensitive intellectual property.
Note: University-provided tools, like Microsoft Copilot (when signed in with your university account), operate under different privacy agreements and offer greater data protection. Check the Tools & Resources page for details.

3. Academic Integrity and Your Thinking

Using AI ethically also means upholding academic integrity. The goal of your education is to develop your own critical thinking, writing, and analytical skills.

  • Don't Outsource Your Brain: Using AI to write an entire essay for you is plagiarism, the same as copying from a website. It violates university policy and bypasses the entire learning process.
  • You Are Responsible: You are 100% responsible for everything you submit. If an AI "hallucinates" a fake reference, makes a factual error, or produces biased content, you are accountable for that mistake.

Refer to the 6-Level AI Framework and your module brief to understand what is permitted for each specific assessment.

4. Environmental Impact

Training and running large AI models requires a significant amount of computational power, which in turn consumes a large amount of energy and water (for cooling data centres).
