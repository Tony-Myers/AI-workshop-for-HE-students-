Your Guide to Permitted AI Use

This 6-level framework is the university's official policy on using Generative AI in your assessed work. It is designed to give you clarity, reduce confusion, and help you develop your skills responsibly.

Always check your module brief. The brief for each specific assignment will tell you which AI level applies (e.g., "This assessment is set at AI Level 3"). If no level is stated, you must assume Level 1 (No AI) and ask your lecturer for clarification.

The Framework Levels

1

Level 1: No AI Permitted

What it means: You must not use any Generative AI tools (like ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini, etc.) to produce, edit, or research any part of your assessment.

This level is used for tasks designed to assess your unassisted knowledge, skills, and critical thinking, such as exams, in-class tests, or personal reflective pieces.

Example tasks: Invigilated exams, viva assessments, reflective logs.

2

Level 2: AI for Development

What it means: You may use AI for general learning and idea generation *before* you start writing your assignment (e.g., to understand a concept or brainstorm topics).

However, you must not use AI to write, edit, or check any text that you submit. The work you submit must be entirely your own.

Example tasks: Essays, reports, or presentations where the focus is on your own writing and analysis.

3

Level 3: AI for Editing

What it means: You may use AI tools to check and improve the surface features of your *own* writing.

This includes checking spelling, grammar, punctuation, and rephrasing for clarity (e.g., "make this sentence more concise"). You must not use it to generate new ideas or content.

Example tasks: Standard coursework, reports. This is often the default for written work.

4

Level 4: AI as Assistant

What it means: You may use AI to assist with generating ideas, finding information, summarising content, or drafting *parts* of your assessment.

You are still responsible for all content, must critically evaluate all AI output, and integrate it into your own work. You must cite the AI and may need to provide evidence of your prompts (check your module brief).

Example tasks: Literature searches, data analysis, drafting initial outlines.

5

Level 5: AI as Collaborator

What it means: AI can be used as a significant collaborator. You can use it to co-create content, analyse complex data, or generate substantial portions of the work.

This level requires a high degree of critical evaluation. You must provide rigorous documentation of your collaboration, including prompts, AI outputs, and your own critical reflections on the process, usually in an appendix.

Example tasks: Research projects, dissertations, coding assignments.

6

Level 6: AI as Expert

What it means: The assessment is *about* the AI itself. You are expected to use AI as a core component of the task, and your critical evaluation of the AI's output *is* the assessment.

You are being marked on your ability to use AI, critique its limitations, analyse its output, and reflect on its role in your discipline. Full documentation and citation are mandatory.

Example tasks: Critiques of AI models, projects to build an AI-driven solution.
