What is the 6-Level Framework?

Birmingham Newman University uses a 6-level framework to clearly communicate what AI use is permitted in each assessment. Every assignment brief will specify which level applies.

Important: Using AI at a level not permitted by your assessment is academic misconduct.

The Six Levels Explained

Level 1: No AI Permitted

What it means: You cannot use AI tools at all for this assessment.

Why: This assessment is testing your unaided abilities.

Documentation required: None (because you are not using AI)

Examples of Level 1 assessments:

  • Exams and in-class tests
  • Practical assessments (coaching sessions, sports therapy treatments)
  • Presentations delivered live
  • Some lab reports (check your brief)
Level 2: AI for Idea Generation Only

What you can do: Use AI to brainstorm topics, generate ideas, or explore concepts.

What you cannot do: Include ANY AI-generated text in your submission. All writing must be your own.

Documentation required: AI Evidence Form describing how you used AI for ideas

Sport Science example:

  • Permitted: Ask AI "What are some interesting topics for a presentation on youth sport development?" then YOU choose and research the topic
  • Not permitted: Asking AI to write any part of your presentation slides
Level 3: AI-Assisted Editing

What you can do: Use AI to improve the clarity, grammar, structure, and academic tone of text YOU have already written.

What you cannot do: Use AI to write new content for you.

Documentation required: AI Evidence Form PLUS your original unedited version in an appendix

Sport Science example:

  • Your original: "Doing plyometric training makes you jump higher because it uses the stretch-shortening cycle which is when muscles stretch then contract quickly."
  • AI editing: "Plyometric training enhances jump performance through exploitation of the stretch-shortening cycle, wherein muscles undergo rapid eccentric-concentric coupling."
  • You submit: The edited version, original version in appendix, and AI Evidence Form
Level 4: AI-Generated Content with Critical Evaluation

What you can do: Ask AI to generate initial content, which you then critically evaluate, verify, and substantially modify.

What you must do: Verify ALL factual claims against academic sources, rewrite in your own words, add critical analysis, cite properly.

Documentation required: Detailed AI Evidence Form explaining your evaluation and modification process

Sport Science example:

  1. Ask AI: "Summarise research on HIIT and VO2max improvements"
  2. AI provides a summary
  3. YOU search SPORTDiscus for actual research studies
  4. YOU read 5-6 primary sources
  5. YOU identify where AI was accurate vs oversimplified
  6. YOU rewrite the section based on actual sources
  7. YOU cite all sources properly
Level 5: AI Collaboration

What this means: AI is used as an ongoing research partner throughout an extended project (e.g., dissertation).

Your role: You maintain intellectual control, make all decisions, verify all content, and demonstrate substantial personal contribution.

Documentation required: Comprehensive AI Evidence Form documenting the entire collaborative process across multiple sessions

Sport Science example:

  • Use AI to help structure your dissertation literature review
  • Upload sources to NotebookLM for pattern identification
  • YOU conduct all literature searches and reading
  • Use AI for feedback on draft sections
  • Iterate over multiple sessions to refine your work
  • Document every stage of collaboration
Level 6: Mandatory AI Use with Comprehensive Documentation

What this means: The assignment REQUIRES you to use AI and evaluate its performance.

Your task: Use AI, document extensively, and critically reflect on AI's strengths, limitations, and ethical implications.

Documentation required: Extremely detailed documentation including full transcripts, critical evaluation, ethical reflection, and recommendations

Sport Science example:

  • Assignment: "Create a digital training resource using AI, then evaluate the AI's contribution"
  • YOU use AI to help create the resource
  • YOU document every AI interaction
  • YOU analyse where AI excelled and where it failed
  • YOU reflect on implications for professional practice

Quick Reference Table

Level What AI Can Do What You Must Do Documentation
Level 1 Nothing - no AI allowed Complete work entirely yourself None
Level 2 Generate ideas/topics only Write everything yourself AI Evidence Form
Level 3 Edit your existing text Write original; AI refines it Form + Original version
Level 4 Generate initial content Evaluate, verify, and rewrite Detailed Form
Level 5 Ongoing collaboration Maintain control; verify all Comprehensive Form
Level 6 Mandated tool Use, document, evaluate Extensive Form + Reflection

Decision Tree: Which Level is My Assignment?

  1. Check your assignment brief - It MUST specify which level is permitted
  2. If no level is specified - Assume Level 1 (no AI) and ask your lecturer
  3. If in doubt - Always ask before submission, not after

Remember: You cannot "upgrade" by documenting better. If your brief permits Level 3, using AI at Level 4 is still misconduct even with perfect documentation.
