Better Questions Get Better Answers

Using an AI is like giving instructions to a very literal person. A vague or broad prompt will get a generic or off-target answer. A well-written, specific prompt will dramatically improve the quality of the AI's output.[1]

General Tips for Effective Prompts

  • Be specific: Clearly state your topic and what you want. "Summarise the key benefits of exercise on mental health" is better than "Tell me about exercise." [1]
  • Provide context: Tell the AI who you are or what you're doing. "I am a sport science student. Help me brainstorm ideas for a nutrition project." [1]
  • State the format: Ask for exactly what you need. "List 3 bullet point ideas..." or "Give the answer in a table with pros and cons." [1]
  • Break it down: For complex tasks, use step-by-step questions. Two or three smaller questions are often better than one giant one, as AI can lose focus.[1]
  • Use examples: Show the AI what you want. "Explain X in a way a 12-year-old would understand." or "Generate a sample reference list entry for a journal article (Newman Harvard style)." [1]
  • Double-check facts: Always verify statistics or references given by the AI. It might "bluff." Cross-verify any sources it provides.[1]
  • Refine and ask again: Prompting is an iterative process. If the answer is wrong, refine your prompt, add detail, or tell the AI what you actually needed.[1]

Prompt Examples for Common Scenarios

Brainstorming Ideas (Level 2)

Good Prompt: "I’m looking for unique essay topics in sport psychology, especially related to motivation techniques for young athletes. Can you suggest 3 possible research questions?” [1]

Improving Writing (Level 3)

Good Prompt: “Improve the clarity and academic tone of the following paragraph about exercise and mental health, without adding new information: [paste your paragraph here].” [1]

Research Help (Level 4)

Good Prompt: “Summarise the consensus from recent research (2015-2023) on high-intensity interval training (HIIT) effects on cardiovascular fitness. Provide 3 key findings and mention the source (if possible).” [1]

Refining an Answer

Good Follow-up Prompt: “The answer you gave is focusing on general fitness. Can you narrow it to focus specifically on mental health benefits, and give me an example study result?” [1]

Learning to write good prompts saves time and makes AI a much more powerful tool. This is a key skill for your university work and beyond.[1]
