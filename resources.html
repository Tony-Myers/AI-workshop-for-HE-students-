Core AI Tools (University Recommended)

These are the primary, university-approved tools for your academic work. They are free to use with your university login and offer enhanced data protection.

Microsoft Copilot (General)

  • What it does: A general-purpose AI assistant for text generation, brainstorming, and editing.
  • Best for: Quick questions, image generation, and general writing help.
  • Permitted at: All levels (depending on the task).

NotebookLM (from Google)

  • What it does: A source-grounded AI that only works with documents you upload.
  • Best for: Summarising academic sources, literature analysis, and research organisation.
  • Why it's safer: Because it is source-grounded, it only uses *your* documents. It cannot "hallucinate" facts or cite sources that do not exist.
  • Permitted at: All levels (Levels 3-6 for academic work).

Custom Copilot Agents

These are specialized AI agents that must be added to the main Copilot interface for best use.

Academic Rigour Assistant

  • What it does: Provides evidence-based, carefully considered responses for academic work.
  • Best for: Understanding research evidence, checking accuracy, and exploring limitations.
  • Perm---itted at: Levels 3-6.

Socratic Tutor

  • What it does: Helps you learn by asking you questions, rather than giving direct answers.
  • Best for: Understanding difficult concepts and developing critical thinking.
  • Permitted at: All levels (as a learning tool).

Research & Verification Databases

AI can generate content, but only these sources can verify it. You must use these to find high-quality academic sources.

SPORTDiscus

  • What it does: A specialist database for sport science and sport medicine research.
  • Best for: Finding peer-reviewed sport science research.
  • Access: Through the university library website.

Google Scholar

  • What it does: A broad search engine for academic research.
  • Best for: Quick searches, finding specific articles, and checking citations.
  • Access: Free, no login required.

Documentation & Forms

AI Evidence Form

  • What it is: The official form for documenting AI use in your assessments.
  • When to use: Required for ALL assessments that permit AI use (Levels 2-6).
  • Availability: Form available on your module's Canvas page or from your lecturer.
