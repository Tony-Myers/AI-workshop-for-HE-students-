AI Tools for Sport Science Students

Microsoft Copilot (General)

Access: copilot.microsoft.com

Login: Use your Birmingham Newman Microsoft 365 account

Best for:

  • Brainstorming ideas and topics
  • Creating outlines and structures
  • Editing and improving clarity
  • General research assistance
  • Understanding complex concepts

Important notes:

  • Custom agents only work in the main Copilot interface, not in Word/Outlook pop-ups
  • Always fact-check information from Copilot
  • Remember which assessment level permits Copilot use

Academic Rigour Assistant (Custom Agent)

Access: Add Academic Rigour Assistant

Purpose: Provides evidence-based, cautious responses with academic rigour

Best for:

  • Fact-checking claims and statistics
  • Understanding research evidence
  • Evaluating the strength of arguments
  • Getting balanced, academic perspectives
  • Verifying information for assignments

Example prompts:

  • "What is the evidence for high-intensity interval training improving VO2max?"
  • "What are the limitations of this claim?"
  • "Evaluate the strength of evidence for creatine supplementation"

Socratic Tutor (Custom Agent)

Access: Add Socratic Tutor

Purpose: Helps you learn through guided questions rather than direct answers

Best for:

  • Understanding difficult concepts
  • Working through problems step-by-step
  • Developing critical thinking
  • Preparing for exams
  • Exploring topics deeply

Example prompts:

  • "I do not understand the difference between aerobic and anaerobic energy systems"
  • "Help me understand how muscles adapt to resistance training"
  • "I am confused about Bayesian statistics in JASP"
How it works: Instead of giving you direct answers, the Socratic Tutor will ask you questions to help you think through the problem yourself. This develops deeper understanding.

NotebookLM (Google)

Access: Go to NotebookLM

Login: Use a Google account (personal or create one for free)

Best for:

  • Summarising research articles
  • Comparing multiple sources
  • Creating audio overviews for revision
  • Source-grounded research (less likely to "hallucinate")
  • Literature review support

Key advantage: Only uses documents YOU upload, provides citations showing exactly where information came from

Quick start:

  1. Click "+ New notebook"
  2. Click "+ Add Source" and upload a PDF or paste text
  3. Ask questions about your sources
  4. Generate summaries or audio overviews
  5. Click citation numbers [1] [2] to see source

SPORTDiscus Database

Access: Via Library A-Z Databases (search for "SPORTDiscus")

Purpose: Premier database for sport science research

Best for finding:

  • Peer-reviewed sport science articles
  • Exercise physiology research
  • Biomechanics studies
  • Sports psychology research
  • Coaching and training studies

Search tips:

  • Use specific terms: "plyometric training" not just "training"
  • Combine concepts with AND: "strength training AND elderly"
  • Use quotes for exact phrases: "maximal oxygen uptake"
  • Tick "peer-reviewed" box for quality sources
  • Limit by date (last 5-10 years for current research)

Data Analysis & Statistics

JASP Statistical Software

Download: JASP Website

Purpose: Free, user-friendly software for statistical analysis

Use for:

  • T-tests and ANOVAs
  • Correlation and regression
  • Bayesian statistics
  • Lab report data analysis
  • Research project analysis

Using AI with JASP:

  • Allowed: Using AI to understand which test to use
  • Allowed: Using AI to interpret JASP output
  • Check assignment brief: Some calculations may be Level 1 (no AI)
  • Always: Include JASP output in your report
  • Always: Verify AI advice against JASP documentation

Example AI prompts for JASP:

  • "I have pre-test and post-test scores from 30 participants. What test should I use in JASP?"
  • "How do I interpret a Bayes Factor of 3.5 in a t-test?"
  • "Explain the assumptions I need to check for repeated measures ANOVA"

Assessment Type: Reflective Writing

Using AI for Reflections

Check your assignment brief first! Reflections are often Level 1 (no AI) or Level 3 (editing only)

If Level 1 (No AI):

  • Write entirely in your own words
  • Reflect on YOUR actual experiences
  • Do not use AI at any stage

If Level 3 (Editing):

  • Write your reflection yourself based on real experiences
  • Can use AI to improve clarity
  • Must include original version in appendix
  • AI cannot add content or change meaning
Never: Have AI generate fake experiences or reflections. Your reflection must be authentic to YOU.

Assessment Type: Presentations & Vivas

Using AI for Presentations

Check your assignment brief! Different presentations have different AI levels.

For SPU412 (Levels 2-3):

  • AI for brainstorming structure (Level 2)
  • AI for editing slides (Level 3)
  • Must complete AI Evidence Form
  • See SPU412 guide

For SPU514 (Level 6):

  • Must use AI throughout
  • Must document everything
  • See SPU514 guide

Viva preparation with AI:

  • Practice questions with Socratic Tutor
  • Anticipate potential questions about your work
  • Prepare explanations of key concepts
  • Remember: In the viva YOU must demonstrate understanding
