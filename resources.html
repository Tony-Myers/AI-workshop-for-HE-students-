This page contains all the AI tools and resources you need for your studies. Click on the links to access each tool.

Core AI Tools

Microsoft Copilot (General)

What it does: General-purpose AI assistant for text generation, brainstorming, and editing.

Best for: Quick questions, image generation, general writing help

Permitted at: All levels (depending on task)

Key features:

  • Image generation (with Dall-E)
  • General Q&A
  • Text drafting and editing
  • Brainstorming ideas
Open Microsoft Copilot

NotebookLM

What it does: Source-grounded AI that works only with your uploaded documents.

Best for: Summarising academic sources, literature analysis, research organisation

Permitted at: All levels (Levels 3-6 for academic work)

Key features:

  • Citations to exact source locations
  • No hallucinations (only uses your documents)
  • Audio summary generation
  • Multi-source synthesis

Why it is safer: Because it only uses YOUR documents, it cannot make up facts or cite sources that do not exist.

Open NotebookLM

Custom Copilot Agents

Important: You must add these agents before you can use them. Click each link to add to your Copilot.

Academic Rigour Assistant

What it does: Provides evidence-based, carefully considered responses for academic work.

Best for: Understanding research evidence, checking accuracy, exploring limitations

Permitted at: Levels 3-6

Key features:

  • Evidence-first approach
  • Acknowledges uncertainties and limitations
  • Uses appropriate academic language
  • Sport science aware

Example use: "What is the research evidence for foam rolling improving recovery?"

Add Academic Rigour Assistant

Socratic Tutor

What it does: Helps you learn by asking questions rather than giving answers directly.

Best for: Understanding difficult concepts, developing critical thinking

Permitted at: All levels (as a learning tool)

Key features:

  • Guides you to answers through questions
  • Encourages active thinking
  • Identifies knowledge gaps
  • Builds understanding, not just answers

Example use: "I do not understand the difference between Type I and Type II muscle fibres."

Add Socratic Tutor

Academic Feedback Assistant

What it does: Converts marker notes into structured, student-facing feedback.

Best for: Lecturers providing consistent, formatted feedback to students

Note: This tool is primarily for staff, not students

Key features:

  • Formats marker notes into professional feedback
  • Adds institutional information
  • Ensures consistency
  • Saves administrative time
Add Academic Feedback Assistant

Research Databases

SPORTDiscus

What it does: Specialist database for sport science and sport medicine research.

Best for: Finding peer-reviewed sport science research

How to access: Through the university library website (requires login)

Why use it: More reliable than general Google searches; peer-reviewed academic sources

Access via Library

Google Scholar

What it does: Search engine for academic research across all disciplines.

Best for: Quick searches, finding specific articles, checking citations

How to access: Free, no login required

Tip: Use the "Cited by" feature to find more recent research on the same topic

Open Google Scholar

Documentation & Forms

AI Evidence Form

What it is: The official form for documenting your AI use in assessments.

When to use: Required for ALL assessments that permit AI use (Levels 2-6)

What to include:

  • Which AI tools you used
  • What prompts you gave the AI
  • Which sections of your work involved AI
  • How you verified or modified AI outputs

Important: Incomplete forms may be treated as academic misconduct.

Form available on your module's Canvas page or from your lecturer.
